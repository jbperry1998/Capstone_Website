The Case for AI in Healthcare

Why medical professionals should be educating themselves on the benefits of new AI technology in healthcare

What Is AI

Artificial Intelligence describes programs that draw conclusions from information, emulating human reasoning

Some common applications include:

  • Self Driving Cars
  • Facial Recognition
  • Chat Bots
  • Natural Language Processing

These technologies are trained using large datasets until they can produce results at a high level

Common Misconceptions About AI

There are a lot of myths and misconceptions about articifial intelligence and what it can and can't do

  • 01 AI Will Not Take Your Job

    One of the most common worries about AI is that it will put people out of jobs. The truth is that AI systems are designed to support people in their roles and make them more efficient.

  • 02 AI Cannot Learn On Its Own

    One of the basic priniciples of AI systgems is that they have to be trained in order to make decisions. This means that they cannot learn new techniques or information on their own.

  • 03 AI Cannot Always Be Objective

    It's important to understand that all AI systems produce results baised off of training data. This means that the results are only as good as the data itself, so these results must alwasy be interpreted in context.

  • 03 AI Cannot Process Informaiton at the Level of a Human Brain

    The term Artificial Intelligence leads you to believe that these systems can think for themselves in the same way that we can. In reality, while some components are designed to mimic some of our brain functions, they are still programs that make finite decisions based on training data.

 

Limitations of AI technologies that need to be considered

There are also some important limitations to the results that AI produces, and these factors are important to know alongside the misconceptions above

  • Only as good as the data: Like mentioned above, the results of these systems are only as good as the data. This is important when training the technology and interpreting the results
  • Data Bias: Similarly, bad data can result in data bias, skewing results one way or another
  • Cost: Like any computer system, AI takes power and bandwidth to run. This can be especially costly for the training and development stage.

Why It's Important for Doctors to Learn More

Clearly AI has and will continue to have a significant impact on the healthcare industry, but until medical professionals are well educated on the subject it can only do so much.

  • New Regulations

    Because of how new this field is and with the complex and sensitive nature of health data, new regualtions need to be put in place to make sure the patients best interests are kept. Doctors need to be educated participants in this conversation to ensure it goes in the right direction.

  • Patient Care Standards

    Doctors have a responsibility to give their patients the best care possible, and at this point that includes using these technologies in some situations.

  • Interpreting Results

    AI generated results will become more and more common in hospitals. Whether doctors interact with the technologies directly or not they may have to interpret the results at some point, and they should have some knowledge of the technology to do that correctly.
